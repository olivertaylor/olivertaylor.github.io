Oliver's Favorite L.A. Restuarants

Here in Los Angeles you can get some of the best food in the world, and no matter where you are in the city there are thousands of wonderful restaurants near you. This guide is a list of my favorite places (I’ve been to all of them), organized by neighborhood.

  • Burbank / The Valley
  • Atwater / Silverlake
  • Hollywood
  • Westside
  • Downtown
  • Inland / Other

Burbank / The Valley

Porto's Bakery

They call themselves a cuban bakery, but this place is great for breakfast, lunch, and a coffee break. Sandwiches, soups, baked-goods, and deserts. Try the Cubano sandwich, the potato balls, and any of their deserts. I'm here maybe 3 times a week.

The Rustic Spoon

A tiny little Thai-fusion place hardly anyone knows about. Great food. I like the cinnamon curry and any of their noodle dishes.

Not Busy $$ Map it ➔

Tomo Sushi

I haven't been to too many of the truly fancy sushi places in LA, but this is (so far) my favorite.

Not Busy $$ Map it ➔

Olive & Thyme

Not only a great breakfast and lunch place, but also serves the best coffee this side of the hill. A little on the pricy side, but worth it for the great food and the wonderful atmosphere. My wife says "it's like an Anthropology in here!"

Not too busy $$$ Map it ➔

Joe's Falafel

A new favorite of mine. Consistently wonderful Mediterranean food; falafel, shawarma, hummus, and don't miss the (baked to order) laffa bread.

Not too busy $ Map it ➔

Jinya Ramen Bar

There are many ramen spots in LA, and the best are on the West Side, which means I don't get to eat at them very often. This gem serves some of my favorite ramen, is always packed, and is now a staple in my diet. With each bowl of ramen they'll ask if you want skinny or fat noodles, get the fat.

Busy $$ Map it ➔

Atwater / Silverlake

Proof Bakery

A great place to get coffee and a pastry, worth a trip if you don't live nearby. Don't have many tables, can be busy.

Busy $$

Mother Dough

Of all the fancy pizza in L.A. (and there is a lot) this place is unique for a few reasons; the dough is phenomenal, it's not a white-tablecloth kind of place, and they don't get ridiculous with what's on the pizza.

Busy-ish $$$

Tomato Pie

A great lunch-pizza place. Some of their more exotic pizzas are very good.

Not too busy $$

Hollywood

Stout

A bar/burger joint, makes one of the best burgers I've ever had. But some people don't like it at all. One of those places you can't ask for customizations, they serve what's on the menu, that's it. Lunch service is great but I've had bad luck with dinner.

Busy $$

Umami Burger

There's a lot of these around town. Pasadena, Westside, and soon Burbank. They make a great burger, simple as that.

Busy-ish $$

Tender Greens

I like to think of this as a "Salad and Protein" joint. I always leave satisfied and it's always healthy. Be prepared to wait 15 minutes in line to order, but you'll sit-down immediately after that.

Busy $$

Joe's Pizza

"Street Pizza". Get the "Grandma Pie", it will restore your faith in humanity.

Not Busy $

Star of India

Honestly, I haven't eaten at a lot of Indian restaurants here in L.A., but this one has great quality food (unlike some nearby places), and it's delicious.

Not Busy $$

Palms Thai

Go for the 4'5" Thai Elvis impersonator (who is really quite good) and the 12" Elvis statue, stay for the garlic tofu. Great food, very strange place. It's fun.

Busy-ish $$

Mao’s Kitchen

My favorite Chinese food in the city.

Not too busy $$

Terroni

A great place to go when you want to feel like you live in the city, not so great if you like a quiet meal. I'm not sure why but this is possibly the loudest restaurant I've ever been in. But it's worth it because the food is really great and it's a lot of fun.

Busy $$$

Mozza Pizzaria

The most famous fancy-pizza place in town - deservedly so. Tough to get a reservation for dinner or lunch on the same day.

Busy $$$

Little Dom's

Nice little Italian place that has a wonderful atmosphere, perfect for a date.

Busy $$

Grub

Frankly this place is strange. Tucked behind aging sound-stages, in what used to be someone's house, this place serves healthy food and delicious bacon.

Not too busy $$

Stella Barra Pizzaria

New semi-fancy pizza place in a location that's cursed the last few restaurants that went in. The pizza is good quality, there's a nice variety, and it's close to the Arclight.

Busy-ish $$

Food Lab

Sandwich shop, spectacular ingredients, great atmosphere, and some very interesting food. Don't be turned-off by the (fairly) ostentatious presentation [it's the kind of place that would list the farms their ingredients came from and the waiters would scoff if you asked for sweet-and-low].

Busy-ish $$

Bludso’s BBQ

Let's leave aside arguments about what kind of barbecue is best (I'm sure the one you grew up with is the best) and I'll just say that of the barbecue I've had in Los Angeles, this is one of the better ones.

Busy-ish $$

Westside

A-Frame

Super hip, super L.A., super delicious. Small plates to share, ate mostly with your hands, the appetizer they're known for is kettle-corn served on a sheet of paper, their famous entre is beer-can chicken - whatever that means, it was delicious.

Busy $$$ Map it ➔

Tsujita LA Artisan Noodle Annex

Quite simply, the best ramen I've ever had. No matter where you're coming from, it's worth the trip, and worth the hour wait once you get your name on the list. Be prepared to wait in a parking lot on a lawn chair, be prepared to order in the parking lot, be prepared for a long wait, be prepared to have your mind blown.

Busy $$ Map it ➔

Tatsu Ramen

There's a lot of Ramen joints around L.A., this is one of my favorites on the Westside. Order on an iPad, then sit down and get excited.

Busy-ish $$

Bay Cities

A bustling deli sandwich shop, famous for their is enormous, and delicious, sandwiches.

Busy $

Joe's Pizza

"Street Pizza". Get the "Grandma Pie", it will restore your faith in humanity.

Not too busy $

Milo & Olive

Another very interesting, very delicious, and very L.A. place. There are about 4 tables, all of them shared with other guests, and the food is like a chef from a fancy restaurant came to your house.

Busy $$$

Downtown

Wurstkuche

Sausage, beer, dance music.

Busy $$

Bottega Louie

Order full entres, pizza, or plates to share. Very hip, very nice place. I once saw (on the same night) a woman in head-to-toe leather and a black tutu and a guy wearing an eyepatch. My wife and I like to go here before concerts, dances, etc.

Busy $$$ Map it ➔

Baco Mercat

A fascinating little place that feels half restaurant and half bar. Specializes in flatbreads folded up into sandwich/taco/buns. Delicious.

Busy-ish $$

Inland / Other

La Cabanita Montrose

I grew up in New Mexico, so it's not that I have a high bar for Mexican food, it's that I can spot the fakers easily. This place is the real deal. Simple, decidedly not fancy, a hole-in-the-wall, with flat-out amazing food. The waiters have all worked there for a long time, ensure you ask them what they recommend, and take it seriously - don't just order enchiladas.

Busy-ish $$

Din Tai Fung San Gabriel

What little I know about Asian food in general, I know less about dumplings. This place is an institution, on every best-of-LA list. A friend took me here a long time ago and I've been going back ever since for the juicy pork dumplings. Be prepared to wait for 30 minutes.

Busy $

Luscious Dumplings San Gabriel

I was down in San Gabriel shooting a movie and a friend of mine (a food lover) took one look at the on-set catering and said "I've got a place." I've learned to trust him blindly when he says things like this - and I swear to God the pan-fried dumpings I ate from this place were one of the most magical things I've eaten. There's a line around the block sometimes and they close the doors when they sell out of food. Lunch only, I think.

Busy $

Golden Deli San Gabriel

Another San Gabriel institution. I don't really know what to say about it other than I've been here, it's delicious.

Busy $$

D'Angelo's Bread Santa Barbara

For some reason I always find myself driving through Santa Barbara instead of staying there, and for some reason I'm always driving through 10am or 4pm, both perfect times for coffee and some food. This place has become something of an oasis for me, great coffee and great pastries.

Not busy $$
